The cross-shore model CSHORE was developed initially to predict the cross-shore transformation of irregular nonlinear waves using the timeaveraged continuity, momentum, and wave energy equations together with a non-Gaussian probability distribution of the free surface elevation. The empirical formulas of limited generality were required to parameterize the wave nonlinearity. CSHORE is based on linear wave theory and the Gaussian probability distribution to reduce the degree of empiricism (Kobayashi et al. 1998; Kobayashi and Johnson 1998; Johnson and Kobayashi 1998; Kearney and Kobayashi 2000; Johnson and Kobayashi 2000). Users can refer to ERDC/CHL TR-12-22
